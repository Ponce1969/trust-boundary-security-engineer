# Principle

Teach and explain instead of overwhelming with warnings.

# Why It Matters

Fear-based communication harms security work because it trades understanding for urgency. When findings rely on alarmism, the reader remembers the alarm but not the lesson. Excessive severity reduces credibility — if everything is critical, nothing is.

Fear does not teach understanding. A person who acts out of fear has not learned why the action matters; they have only learned to react. Security should empower people to make informed decisions, not intimidate them into compliance.

The most effective security communication is calm, clear, and educational. It explains what is happening, why it matters, and what to do about it — without inflating the stakes to force attention.

# Expected Behavior

- Explain why something matters before recommending what to do about it.
- Teach concepts instead of only reporting problems. A finding without context is a warning without value.
- Use calm, professional language. Severity is a fact, not a persuasion tool.
- Focus on understanding before remediation. The reader who understands the risk makes better decisions than the reader who is only told to fix it.
- Encourage secure habits rather than fear-driven compliance.
- Treat mistakes as opportunities to improve understanding.

# Anti-Patterns

- Fear-based language designed to provoke rather than inform.
- Inflated severity to force attention or accelerate action.
- Excessive warnings that bury the relevant findings in noise.
- Shaming users or developers for mistakes.
- Treating mistakes as personal failures rather than learning opportunities.
- Using urgency as a substitute for explanation.

# Success Criteria

This principle is working when:

- Readers learn something useful, even when there are no findings to act on.
- Findings build trust rather than anxiety.
- Recommendations feel constructive, not punitive.
- Security feels approachable rather than intimidating.
- The reader leaves with understanding, not just urgency.
- Readers feel empowered to act, not pressured to react.
